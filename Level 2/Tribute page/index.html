Champion of Nonviolence and Freedom

About Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, also known as Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, was a pivotal figure in the Indian independence movement. His approach to nonviolent resistance and civil disobedience inspired millions and left a lasting legacy.

Biography

Born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India, Gandhi studied law in London and became a lawyer. His experiences in South Africa shaped his philosophy of nonviolence. Returning to India, he led numerous campaigns for social reform and political freedom, culminating in the Indian independence movement.

Notable events include the Salt March of 1930 and the Quit India Movement of 1942, which played critical roles in India's struggle for independence. Gandhi's methods of peaceful protest had a global impact, influencing future movements for civil rights and social justice.

Legacy

Gandhi's principles of nonviolence and his dedication to social justice continue to inspire leaders and activists worldwide. His teachings emphasize the power of peaceful resistance and remain relevant in global struggles for justice and equality.
